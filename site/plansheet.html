1.4 AI Phone Call Outreach (TCPA-Compliant)
P0 Growth Phase 1
▾
The feature no competitor has built. After email outreach, an AI voice agent calls every off-platform supplier with a structured RFQ presentation. Uses press-1 opt-in to stay TCPA-compliant. Runs 24/7, zero human labor.
🔒
TCPA Compliance Framework
All calls use press-1 opt-in. Max 1 call per supplier per day. Full call log with timestamp, duration, and consent record. Opt-out honored immediately and stored permanently.
Bland.ai / Vapi Twilio call_id outcome (opted_in / voicemail / no_answer) call_duration_secs opted_out_at Auto-dial on RFQ post Re-call for negotiation round
1.5 Split-Bid Auto-Negotiation Engine
P0 Revenue Phase 1
▾
When multiple bids are received, the AI identifies the cheapest combination across suppliers by line item. It then calls the losing suppliers on specific line items and attempts to get them to sharpen pricing — creating a Dutch auction effect. Result: 12–22% average savings vs. best single bid.
Algorithm
1. Parse all bids into line items
2. Find cheapest supplier per line item
3. Calculate optimal split combination
4. Identify "close second" items
5. Trigger negotiation calls on those items
6. Re-optimize after negotiation responses
Metrics Tracked
Avg savings vs. single best bid12–22%
Negotiation conversion rateTarget 35%
Avg bids per RFQTarget 4+

05 · Risk Register

What Could Go Wrong &
How We Mitigate It

Scored by Probability × Impact (1–5 each). Anything above 12 is tracked weekly.

16
THIN SUPPLY — Not enough suppliers to deliver 3+ bids per RFQ
The core product promise breaks if contractors post and get 1–2 bids. Suppliers are inherently harder to acquire than contractors. P=4, I=4.
→ Mitigation: Off-platform Google Maps outreach is the primary counter. Pre-seed 50+ suppliers in RI before first contractor onboards. Track avg bids/RFQ daily. Halt contractor expansion if it drops below 2.5.
15
TCPA VIOLATION — AI calling triggers regulatory action
TCPA fines are $500–$1,500 per call per violation. A class action from even 100 suppliers = $50K–$150K exposure. P=3, I=5.
→ Mitigation: Press-1 opt-in architecture (never outbound without consent signal). Max 1 call/supplier/day. Full call log retained. Legal review of call scripts before launch. Opt-out list enforced at DB level, not app level.
12
SEO DISRUPTION — Google algorithm change de-ranks supplier pages
If Google classifies auto-generated supplier pages as thin content, organic traffic collapses. P=3, I=4.
→ Mitigation: Enrich pages with real transaction data (prices, reviews, response rates) ASAP. Claimed pages get supplier-added content. Diversify traffic via autoblog, Reddit/social listening, and paid to reduce SEO dependency below 40%.
9
PROCORE ENTRY — Procore launches competitive procurement feature
Procore has $1B+ ARR and deep contractor relationships. If they launch a procurement module with off-platform outreach, they can out-distribute us. P=2, I=4 (they move slowly).
→ Mitigation: 18-month window before they could ship anything comparable. Win enough contractors and suppliers fast enough that switching cost is real. AI negotiation + split-bid are hard to replicate. Focus on SMB contractors Procore ignores.
8
AI HALLUCINATION — Voice RFQ misinterprets material specs
Contractor says "I need 2x4s" and AI structures it as 2×4×8 when they meant 2×4×12. Wrong material ordered. P=4, I=2.
→ Mitigation: Mandatory confirmation screen shows AI-parsed RFQ before submission. Contractor must explicitly approve each line item. Dispute resolution flow for wrong-spec deliveries. Supplier and contractor both confirm specs at deal closure.
4
PAYMENT FRAUD — Escrow funds misdirected in Phase 3
Payment fraud via fake supplier accounts. Low probability with Stripe KYC but non-zero. P=1, I=4.
→ Mitigation: Stripe Connect with full KYC before any supplier can receive payouts. Payments held in escrow until delivery confirmed. Contractor confirms receipt before funds release. Max payout delay of 7 days for new suppliers.
